FACILITATOR GUIDE

The Battle Is the Lord’s

1 Samuel 17–18; 24–26; 2 Samuel 5–7

Your Role as Facilitator

Your primary goal is to help class members feel comfortable sharing what they learned, felt, and were prompted to change while studying this week. You are a guide, not a lecturer. Listen more than you speak. Celebrate every sincere comment. Trust the Spirit.

Opening (5–8 minutes)

Read the introduction paragraph from the manual:

“Ever since the tribes of Israel had settled in the promised land, the Philistines had been an ongoing threat to their safety. The Lord had delivered them many times in the past, but now the elders of Israel demanded, ‘We will have a king … [to] go out before us, and fight our battles’ (1 Samuel 8:19–20). So Saul was anointed king. And yet when the menacing giant Goliath hurled his challenge to the armies of Israel, Saul—like the rest of his army—was ‘greatly afraid’ (1 Samuel 17:11). On that day, it wasn’t King Saul who saved Israel but a humble shepherd boy named David, who was wearing no armor but was clothed with impenetrable faith in the Lord. This battle proved to Israel, and to anyone who has spiritual battles to fight, that ‘the Lord saveth not with sword and spear’ and that ‘the battle is the Lord’s’ (1 Samuel 17:47).”

Suggested Opening Question:

“Before we dive into discussion, I’d love to hear from you: What did you do to study Come, Follow Me this week? There are so many different ways to study, and I’m always inspired when I hear how others are bringing the gospel into their daily lives.”

1. David and Goliath — “The Battle Is the Lord’s”

1 Samuel 17 • Key verses: 17:26, 32–37, 45–47

Discussion Prompts

  • • What do you notice about David’s confidence compared to how Saul and the army respond?
  • • How does remembering past deliverances (lion & bear) help David face Goliath?
  • • What “giants” are people facing right now? How can past blessings give courage?

Application Question

“What ‘giant’ are you facing right now, and how might remembering the Lord’s past help change how you approach it?”

2. Relationships, Jealousy & Christlike Love

1 Samuel 18 • Key verses: 18:1–4

Discussion Prompts

  • • Contrast Saul’s reaction to David’s success with Jonathan’s reaction.
  • • How is Jonathan’s willingness to yield his future throne a type of Christ?
  • • How can we better rejoice in others’ blessings instead of feeling jealous?

3. Mercy, Forgiveness & Wisdom

1 Samuel 24–26 • Key verses: 24:6, 25:24–31

Discussion Prompts

  • • David had two clear opportunities for revenge. Why did he choose mercy both times?
  • • How is Abigail a type of Christ?
  • • When have you felt prompted to show mercy instead of seeking revenge?

Application Question

“Is there a situation right now where the Lord might be inviting you to lay down a vengeful heart?”

4. Seeking Divine Direction & the Everlasting Covenant

2 Samuel 5 & 7

Discussion Prompts

  • • Notice how David repeatedly “inquired of the Lord.” How can we do this more?
  • • How does the Davidic covenant point us to Jesus Christ?

Closing

Invite sharing:

“After our discussion today, what is one thing you feel prompted to do or change this week?”

Optional: Sing “Let Us All Press On” (Hymns #243)
