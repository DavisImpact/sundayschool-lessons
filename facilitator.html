FACILITATOR

The Battle Is the Lord’s

1 Samuel 17–18; 24–26; 2 Samuel 5–7

KEY THEME
The battle is the Lord’s — victory comes through faith and trust in Him, not human strength.
OPENING QUESTION
“What did you do to study Come, Follow Me this week?”
CLOSING INVITATION
What do you feel prompted to do or change this week?

Opening

Read the introduction paragraph from the manual:

“Ever since the tribes of Israel had settled in the promised land, the Philistines had been an ongoing threat... On that day, it wasn’t King Saul who saved Israel but a humble shepherd boy named David... This battle proved... that the battle is the Lord’s.”

Suggested opening question:

“What did you do to study Come, Follow Me this week? I’d love to hear how you studied and what it was like.”

1. David and Goliath — “The Battle Is the Lord’s”

1 Samuel 17 • Key verses: 17:26, 32–37, 45–47

Discussion Prompts:

  • • What do you notice about David’s confidence compared to the army’s fear?
  • • How does remembering past deliverances (lion & bear) help David face Goliath?
  • • What “giants” are people facing right now? How can past blessings give courage?

Application Question:

“What ‘giant’ are you facing right now, and how might remembering the Lord’s past help change how you approach it?”

2. Relationships, Jealousy & Christlike Love

1 Samuel 18 • Key verses: 18:1–4

Discussion Prompts:

  • • Contrast Saul’s reaction to David’s success with Jonathan’s reaction.
  • • How is Jonathan’s willingness to yield his future throne a type of Christ?
  • • How can we better rejoice in others’ blessings instead of feeling jealous?

3. Mercy, Forgiveness & Wisdom

1 Samuel 24–26 • Key verses: 24:6, 25:24–31

Discussion Prompts:

  • • Why do you think David chose mercy when he had the chance for revenge?
  • • How is Abigail a type of Christ?
  • • When have you felt prompted to show mercy instead of seeking revenge?

Application Question:

“Is there a situation right now where the Lord might be inviting you to lay down a vengeful heart?”

4. Seeking Divine Direction & the Everlasting Covenant

2 Samuel 5 & 7

Discussion Prompts:

  • • Notice how David repeatedly “inquired of the Lord.” How can we do this more?
  • • How does the Davidic covenant point us to Jesus Christ?

Closing

Invite sharing:

“After our discussion today, what is one thing you feel prompted to do or change this week?”

Consider singing “Let Us All Press On” (Hymns #243) if time allows.
