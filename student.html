This Week’s Lesson

The Battle Is the Lord’s

1 Samuel 17–18; 24–26 • 2 Samuel 5–7

God fights our battles

This week we see how the Lord delivers His people — not through human strength or impressive weapons, but through faith, humility, and trust in Him. David’s story reminds us that the battles we face (big or small) are ultimately the Lord’s.

Key Moments in the Story

1 SAMUEL 17

David vs Goliath

A young shepherd boy faces a giant warrior. While the army is afraid, David trusts that “the battle is the Lord’s.”

1 SAMUEL 18 & 24–26

Relationships & Mercy

We see Jonathan’s loyal friendship and David choosing mercy over revenge when he spares King Saul’s life — twice.

Verses Worth Reflecting On

“The Lord saveth not with sword and spear: for the battle is the Lord’s.”

— 1 Samuel 17:47

“The Lord that delivered me out of the paw of the lion, and out of the paw of the bear, he will deliver me out of the hand of this Philistine.”

— 1 Samuel 17:37

Questions to Think About

These questions can help you prepare what you might want to share in class.

01

What “giants” are you facing right now?

How might remembering how God has helped you in the past give you courage?

02

David showed mercy even when he had the chance for revenge.

When have you felt prompted to choose mercy or forgiveness instead of getting even?

03

What stood out to you most while you were reading this week?

Was there a verse, a feeling, or something you want to change?

Come ready to share

You don’t need to have all the answers. Just bring what the Lord taught you this week — even if it’s just one verse or one feeling.
